Weather Prediction

The prediction of the weather forecasts has always been notoriously unreliable, you can often hear it remarked that you should never trust the weather forecast however with the implementation of technology the accuracy and foresight that can be provided has greatly improved.

The first type of weather forecasting was done by sharing information collected in different locations using a telegraph. In 1854 the Meteorological Department or Met Office was founded, the office provided instruments for Royal Navy ships to gather data for computation. 15 stations were set up around the country which provided daily weather reports at set times to the Met Office.

In 1911 the Met Office started issuing marine weather forecasts via Radio Transmission. Later in 1936 the first televised weather forecast was shown on the BBC.

The first weather forecast using a modern computer was in 1950 at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. John von Neumann put together a team of meteorologists and together they used ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator And Computer), the worldâ€™s first general purpose computer, to complete a 24 hour forecast. The forecast took 24 hours to compute including the time taken to load the punch cards however it clearly showed that if the speed of computation could be increased then the future of weather forecasting lied in computers.
